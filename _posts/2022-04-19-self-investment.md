Not being healthy is starting to worry me so I think I should start to do somthing about that

CGP Grey has a great video about New Year's resolutions and why they don't work. Instead, he suggests that we use a theme. For example, someone who wants to improve their education might have a theme of learning. This is broad enough to keep you on track but not shackle you to something like a resolution such as "I will read 2 books a month." That goal is easy to fail and hard to get back on track once you do. With a theme, you have a guiding light to pull you towards where you want to be. Most actions that you take should have a moment of consideration: "Will this take me closer to X theme?" If the answer is yes, you should do it, and if no, you should consider whether it is worth the other benefits.

The crudely drawn graph above shows a trend line towards a goal, even though there are some dips in the actual points on the graph.

All that being said, my theme as of today is **health investment**, and I am giving myself a season (about 3 months) before I review my theme.

Over the past 27 years or so, I have neglected my health, either because I was young and could consume 12 cans of Coke without feeling a thing, I was stressed out and didn't care, or I was not stressed out and didn't care. As I am rapidly approaching my 30s, I am feeling pressure to improve my health and keep my body "young" for as long as possible, and a life of working from bed and eating chocolate buttons is no way to achieve that.

So with all that being said, this is my commitment: I will try to improve the numbers on [this page](https://jakestocker.com/health) and hopefully feel better and be better than I am now in 3 months' time.
